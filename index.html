About Me

I am a PhD graduate from McMaster University. Throughout my doctoral research, I led a project and conducted extensive research on cutting-edge topics such as Automated Vehicles (AVs) and Shared Mobility (SM), alongside delving into current travel behavior in Canadian Census Metropolitan Areas (CMAs). My most recent research explores the complex realm of psychological factors influencing Canadians’ intention to use and financially support unprecedented travel modes, utilizing complex statistical approaches.

In addition to my academic background, I have several years of experience in consulting companies, specializing in Urban and Transportation Planning roles.
